About Me

I'm a CS graduate from Bangkok University with a passion for two seemingly different worlds — data science and game development. But to me, they're deeply connected: both are about understanding systems, finding patterns, and creating meaningful experiences.

My long-term goal is to build living worlds — where every NPC has a real role and daily life, where the environment shifts from the actions of both players and NPCs, and where the world continues to breathe and evolve even when no one is watching. I don't just want to make games. I want to create another world that truly exists. My background in ML gives me the tools to make that possible — because a world that truly lives needs systems that truly think.

Beyond the technical side, I'm drawn to the creative heart of games — designing game systems and maps, building worlds that feel real and reactive, and writing stories that players remember. I believe great games come from people who understand both the data behind player behavior and the emotion behind every design decision — from how an NPC responds to its environment, to how a map guides a player's journey without them noticing.

When I'm not coding, you'll find me playing TCG games, exploring open-world games for inspiration, or crafting stories for my next visual novel.
